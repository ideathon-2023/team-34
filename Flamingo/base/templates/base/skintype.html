1) Normal Skin:

Normal skin is well-balanced and generally has a good texture. It's not too oily or too dry, and the pores are usually small and not easily noticeable. People with normal skin typically have few blemishes and experience minimal sensitivity. Normal skin requires basic skincare maintenance to keep it healthy.

Dry Skin:

Dry skin lacks sufficient moisture, and as a result, it can feel tight, rough, and flaky. It may have visible fine lines and a dull appearance. Dry skin can be caused by environmental factors, genetics, or improper skincare. It needs extra hydration and moisturization to maintain its elasticity and prevent premature aging.

Oily Skin:

Oily skin produces an excess of sebum, the skin's natural oil. This can result in a shiny appearance, especially on the forehead, nose, and chin (the T-zone). Oily skin is prone to enlarged pores, blackheads, and acne breakouts due to the buildup of oil and debris. Proper cleansing and oil-controlling products are essential for managing oily skin.

Combination Skin:

Combination skin is a mix of different skin types on different areas of the face. Typically, the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) is oilier, while the cheeks are drier. Managing combination skin can be tricky, as you'll need to use products that cater to both dry and oily areas of your face.

Sensitive Skin:

Sensitive skin is prone to irritation and reactions when exposed to certain products or environmental factors. It can be characterized by redness, itching, burning, or stinging sensations. People with sensitive skin need gentle, hypoallergenic, and fragrance-free products to avoid triggering adverse reactions.

Mature Skin:

Mature skin refers to skin that has aged and undergone changes due to the natural aging process and external factors like sun exposure and lifestyle choices. It is often drier and may show more signs of wrinkles, fine lines, and age spots. Proper anti-aging skincare and sun protection are crucial for maintaining mature skin.
