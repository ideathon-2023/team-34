Straight Hair:

Straight hair is characterized by hair strands that lie flat and lack natural curls or waves. It is generally smooth and shiny. Straight hair can be fine, medium, or coarse in thickness.

Wavy Hair:

Wavy hair has a slight 'S' shape or loose curls. It falls between straight and curly hair. Wavy hair can range from fine to coarse, and the amount of wave can vary from subtle to more pronounced.

Curly Hair:

Curly hair forms well-defined curls or coils. It can be categorized into three subtypes based on curl pattern: a. Type 2 (Wavy): Loose waves that form a slight 'S' shape. b. Type 3 (Curly): More defined curls ranging from loose to tight ringlets. c. Type 4 (Coily): Very tight, small curls or coils, also known as "kinky" or "afro-textured" hair.

Kinky Hair:

Kinky hair, often referred to as Type 4 hair, has tightly coiled or zigzag-shaped curls. This hair type is common among people of African descent and can be further categorized into Type 4A, 4B, and 4C, based on the curl pattern and texture.

Thin Hair:

Thin hair refers to hair that has a smaller diameter and feels less voluminous. People with thin hair may have less density and struggle with creating volume in their hairstyles.

Thick Hair:

Thick hair has a larger hair diameter and can feel dense and voluminous. It often holds styles well but can be challenging to manage due to its volume.

Fine Hair:

Fine hair refers to the texture of each individual hair strand. It means that each strand has a smaller diameter, regardless of the overall thickness of the hair. Fine hair can be straight, wavy, curly, or kinky.

Coarse Hair:

Coarse hair has a thicker individual hair strand diameter. This hair type may feel rough or wiry to the touch and can be straight, wavy, curly, or kinky.

Normal Hair:

Normal hair falls somewhere between the extremes of fine and coarse, and it is neither too oily nor too dry. It is manageable and doesn't require specialized care.
